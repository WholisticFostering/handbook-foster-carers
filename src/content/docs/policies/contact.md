This policy explains how contact with birth families, relatives and
friends should be arranged, supported and reviewed for children living

-   Contact with parents and siblings
-   Supervised and unsupervised contact
-   Social visits (including overnight stays) with relatives or friends

Children should be supported to **maintain safe and appropriate

Contact arrangements must be:

-   **Approved** by the child's social worker
-   **Clearly recorded** in the Placement Plan
-   **Reviewed regularly** at Looked After Reviews

Contact must **never be withdrawn as a sanction** for behaviour.

observations promptly.

If foster carers are asked to supervise or attend family contact:

-   They must speak to their **supervising social worker first**
-   A **risk assessment** must be completed
-   If at any point the safety of the carer or child is at risk, they
    should **leave and report immediately**

-   Their **supervising social worker**
-   The **child's social worker** within 24 hours

Children should be supported to take part in **normal, age-appropriate
social activities** such as:

-   Sleepovers with friends
-   Weekend trips with other families
-   Occasional childcare by trusted adults
-   School trips

These should be treated **like they would for any other child**, unless
there's a specific risk or restriction.

Foster carers should assess:

-   Any **concerns or risks** in the proposed household?
-   Is the stay with a **child/peer**, not just an adult?
-   Is the **child's age and understanding** appropriate?
-   Is it clear **whose idea** the stay was?
-   Does the child **know and trust** the people involved?
