Wholistic Fostering is committed to **valuing and retaining foster
carers**. Foster carers are central to the success of the agency.
Retaining experienced carers improves outcomes for children and
strengthens the agency's services.

We recognise that carers have choices, and excellent **support, respect,
communication and recognition** are essential to carer satisfaction.

## ğŸ’¡ Our Approach

Carers stay with agencies where they feel:

-   Valued
-   Supported
-   Part of a professional, trusted team

Supervising Social Workers (SSWs) play a key role in this. The
day-to-day support, supervision and responsiveness of SSWs is directly
linked to a carer's sense of stability and confidence.

We promote a team culture where staff and carers attend training
together and work in partnership toward shared goals.

## ğŸ”„ Day-to-Day Retention Practice

Every action matters -- from **returning phone calls** to **recognising
achievements**. Our culture expects attention to detail, warmth, and
professionalism from everyone.

We want carers to:

-   Feel heard
-   Influence positive change (e.g. through a Carer Representative)
-   Receive regular placements
-   Be well-trained and well-matched

## ğŸ“¬ Carer Resignations

Not all resignations are negative --- some carers leave due to
retirement or family changes. However, where concerns exist about a
carer's practice, these must be investigated **before** accepting a
resignation.

We always seek to understand why a carer is leaving. Even seemingly
minor concerns can be important, and carers may hesitate to raise them.
Open discussion can often prevent future resignations.

## ğŸ“˜ Exit Interviews

An **exit interview** will be arranged for every resignation. This helps
us improve the service and understand what led to the decision.

Key points:

-   Interviewed by someone not directly involved with the carer
-   Findings are written up and shared with relevant managers
-   Themes and learning are fed back into agency development
