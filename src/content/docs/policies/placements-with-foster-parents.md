WF only accepts referrals from local authorities. Referrers must

-   Purpose and timescale of placement

follow within 5 working days, or the Independent Reviewing Officer (IRO)

## ğŸ” Matching Process

Referrals are reviewed by WF's Duty Worker and Fostering Manager. We

-   The child's needs (school, health, contact, identity, behaviour)
-   Carer's experience, availability, household makeup
-   Distance from child's school

We won't propose placements if needs cannot be met, even with support. A
Planning Meeting or professionals' discussion may occur for complex
needs.

## ğŸ“ Placement Planning

Before placement (or within 5 days for emergencies), a Planning Meeting

-   Share all relevant info with carers
-   Agree on Placement Plan (including health, education, contact,
    behaviour)
-   Clarify delegated authority and safeguarding actions
-   Discuss independence plans if age-appropriate

The child's social worker must provide:

-   Care Plan
-   Chronology and relevant reports
-   Signed medical consents

Placements may only end for:

-   Planned Care/Pathway Plan reasons
-   To protect the child or others
-   Emergency safeguarding reasons

considered, treat as a new placement. WF will involve the IRO if the
placing authority ends the placement unexpectedly.

Carers are encouraged to stay in touch after children move on.
