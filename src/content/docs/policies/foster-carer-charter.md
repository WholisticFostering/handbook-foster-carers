## ü§ù 1. What Foster Carers Can Expect from Wholistic Fostering

### ü§ù 1.1 Working in Partnership

**Wholistic Fostering will:**

-   recognise that foster carers live with children every day and
    usually know them best
-   value foster carers' skills and expertise equally to those of other
    professionals
-   include foster carers in all meetings that affect them and the
    children they care for (unless safeguarding requirements prevent
    this)
-   comply fully with all fostering regulations and guidance
-   treat foster carers without discrimination and respect them as
    colleagues
-   respect confidentiality

### üìÑ 1.2 Information

**Wholistic Fostering will:**

-   share all information we hold about the child so carers can care
    safely
-   provide this information in writing before placement, or as soon as
    possible for emergencies
-   give clear details of all financial matters (tax, allowances,
    additional entitlements)
-   supply foster carers with all relevant policies and procedures

### üõü 1.3 Support

**Wholistic Fostering will:**

-   provide a robust post-approval induction
-   respond positively to requests for additional help, including
    respite/short breaks and out-of-hours support
-   offer regular supervision and phone contact
-   give honest, open feedback
-   ensure 24-hour access to support from people with fostering
    expertise
-   pay allowances, expenses and fees on time
-   maintain a local support group where carers and their families can
    share experiences
-   support carers when facilitating birth-family contact
-   recognise and champion the contribution foster carers and their
    families make

### üéì 1.4 Learning and Development

**Wholistic Fostering will:**

-   ensure every foster carer has an annual Personal Development Plan
    (within their review)
-   provide appropriate, accessible training delivered by trainers who
    understand fostering
-   support carers in building meaningful relationships with the
    children they look after and the wider team around the child
-   offer wider development opportunities (for example mentoring,
    training delivery or peer support)

### ‚öñÔ∏è 1.5 Fair Treatment

**Wholistic Fostering will:**

-   consult foster carers before changing terms and conditions
-   act with honesty and openness in all communications
-   treat carers with respect, keep them informed and provide emotional
    and practical support if they are subject to an allegation
-   follow a clear framework (with independent support) for dealing with
    allegations and keep to agreed timescales
-   explain arrangements for payment of fees and allowances if carers
    cannot foster while under investigation

### üì¢ 1.6 Communication and Consultation

**Wholistic Fostering will:**

-   consult foster carers meaningfully on matters that affect them
-   provide timely feedback from consultations
-   publish clear routes for carers to give feedback or raise concerns

## üåü 2. What Wholistic Fostering Can Expect from Foster Carers

### ü§ù 2.1 Working in Partnership

Foster carers will:

-   demonstrate expertise and use their skills to the best of their
    ability
-   provide children with a positive family experience
-   attend meetings about the children and young people in their care
-   work collaboratively with the child's social worker, Wholistic
    Fostering, schools, health and faith bodies
-   work constructively with birth parents, wider family and other
    significant people
-   meet all relevant fostering regulations, guidance, policies and
    procedures
-   tell their supervising social worker about changes or issues
    affecting their fostering
-   inform the child's social worker of any concerns about the child
-   respect confidentiality
-   maintain an open dialogue with Wholistic Fostering

### üßí 2.2 Respect for the Child

Foster carers will:

-   build a meaningful relationship with the child, advocate for them
    and aim to stay in touch when they move on (where in the child's
    best interest)
-   care for the child in a way that recognises and respects their
    identity (ethnic, religious, linguistic, cultural)
-   give the same protection and care they would offer their own child
-   support the child to make age-appropriate decisions about their life
-   help the child influence the development of services that affect
    them

### üéì 2.3 Learning, Development and Support

Foster carers will:

-   commit to developing their skills throughout their fostering career
-   attend relevant training
-   participate in and contribute to support groups

## üíú 3. Wholistic Fostering Values

Wholistic Fostering endorses the values set out in the Fostering
Services National Minimum Standards (2011):

-   The child's welfare, safety and needs are central to their care
-   Children should enjoy childhood, experience excellent parenting and
    education, and have opportunities to develop their talents and
    skills for a successful adult life
-   Children are entitled to grow up in a loving environment that meets
    their developmental needs
-   Every child's wishes and feelings should be listened to and taken
    into account
-   Each child should be valued as an individual and given personalised
    support that develops identity, self-confidence and self-worth
-   The particular needs of disabled children and those with complex
    needs will be fully recognised and met
-   The importance of contact with birth parents, wider family and
    siblings is recognised, as is the foster carer's role in supporting
    it
-   Children in foster care deserve the fullest experience of family
    life, with minimum unnecessary restrictions
-   The central importance of the child's relationship with their foster
    carer is acknowledged; foster carers are core team members
-   Foster carers have a right to full information about the child
-   Foster carers must receive the support services and development
    opportunities they need to provide the best care
-   Genuine partnership among everyone involved in fostering is
    essential for achieving the best outcomes for children
