All Wholistic carers:

Supervising social workers offer advice and may provide equipment (e.g.

## ğŸ§¸ Home Safety Essentials

Foster homes must:

-   Be clean, in good repair, and safe throughout
-   Offer safe, age-appropriate space for each child
-   Keep medicines, cleaning products, sharp or hot items safely stored
-   Ensure smoke alarms work and are tested regularly
-   Have secure windows, stair gates (where needed), and socket covers
    for young children
-   Store food hygienically and follow UK food safety standards

## ğŸš¸ Supervision & Awareness

Looked-after children may face increased risks due to:

-   Developmental or behavioural needs
-   Unfamiliarity with house rules or safety hazards
-   Self-harm risks or impulsive behaviours

Foster carers should:

-   Supervise young children closely
-   Provide clear health and safety guidance appropriate to the child\'s
    age and understanding
-   Use safety equipment appropriately indoors and outdoors

## ğŸ”¥ Fire Safety

All homes must have:

-   Working smoke alarms on every floor
-   Night-time fire safety checks (e.g. switch off appliances, close
    doors)
-   A clear fire escape plan known by all household members

Other key points:

-   Keep matches, lighters, candles and electrical items away from
    children
-   Unplug unused items; don't overload sockets
-   Use fireguards and avoid trailing cables
-   Train children in safe fire behaviour (e.g. what to do if there's a
    fire)
