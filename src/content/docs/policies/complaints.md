Wholistic Fostering believes that everyone --- children, carers,
families, and professionals --- should feel safe and supported in
raising concerns or complaints. We are committed to resolving complaints
quickly, fairly, and respectfully.

This policy complies with:

-   Fostering Services National Minimum Standards 2011 -- Standard 1,
    21, and 25
-   Children Act 1989 Representations Procedure
-   Ofsted and Regulatory Authority guidance

## ğŸ™‹ Who Can Make a Complaint?

A complaint can be made by:

-   A child or young person
-   A parent or family member
-   A foster carer
-   A professional involved with the service
-   Any individual receiving services from WF
-   Anyone acting on behalf of another person with a legitimate interest

Children will be supported in making complaints in ways that suit their
age and understanding. Staff and carers are expected to actively support
this.

Anyone can withdraw a complaint at any time.

## âš ï¸ What Can Be Complained About?

Complaints may relate to:

-   The quality or delivery of service
-   The actions or behaviour of staff or carers
-   Failure to provide a service
-   Unfair treatment or discrimination
-   Decisions made by WF that impact on a person's wellbeing or rights

Serious complaints will be reported to the Regulatory Authority
(Ofsted).

If a complaint relates to a **Care Plan or Placement Plan**, the matter
should be raised with the child's **social worker** and/or **Independent
Reviewing Officer**.

## ğŸ“‹ Stages of the Complaints Procedure

WF encourages resolution at the earliest possible stage, but all
complaints are taken seriously. Complainants may start at any stage if
appropriate.

### 1ï¸âƒ£ Stage One: Informal Resolution

-   Handled by the Fostering Manager.
-   A meeting or discussion is arranged after receiving the complaint.
-   A written response (letter of outcome) will be provided, explaining:
    1.  Who handled it
    2.  What was investigated
    3.  What decision was made and why

### 2ï¸âƒ£ Stage Two: Formal Investigation

If unresolved at Stage One --- or the complainant prefers to escalate
directly:

-   A written complaint should be sent to the **Director** of WF.
-   An **Independent Investigating Officer** is appointed within 5
    working days.
-   A full investigation will take place.
-   A written report is sent outlining the investigation and findings.

### 3ï¸âƒ£ Stage Three: Independent Review Panel

If unsatisfied after Stage Two:

-   A request must be made in writing to the Director, with reasons.
-   An **Independent Complaints Panel** will review the case within 28
    days.
-   The Panel includes at least:
    1.  A Fostering Panel member
    2.  A Board Director
    3.  A senior staff member
    4.  An independent individual
-   The complainant receives a written outcome within 7 days of the
    review.

If still dissatisfied, the complainant may contact **Ofsted**.

## ğŸ’· Financial Implications

-   Payments **continue** if children remain in placement during the

    possible.
