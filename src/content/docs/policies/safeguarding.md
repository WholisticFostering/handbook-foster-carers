Every foster home must have a Safe Caring Family Policy in place,
setting out household routines, rules, and boundaries that promote safe,
respectful and nurturing care for all children. It is completed with
support from your Supervising Social Worker and is a requirement before
any child is placed.

## ğŸ“˜ What is a Safe Caring Family Policy?

A short written plan that:

-   Helps keep children safe from harm or abuse
-   Reduces the risk of allegations against foster carers
-   Makes clear how safe caring is practiced in your home

Safe Caring Policies are updated:

-   Before a child is placed
-   With each new placement
-   When household circumstances change
-   Annually as part of your foster carer review

Older children should also be involved where appropriate.

## ğŸ“Œ Key Principles

-   Show warmth, care and respect
-   Avoid confusion about roles (e.g. children should not call carers
    "mum" or "dad")
-   Respect children's personal space and belongings
-   Give clear, age-appropriate messages about body safety, consent and
    boundaries
-   Maintain safe supervision -- especially when children play together
-   Foster carers must **never use corporal punishment**
-   Keep a copy of your policy accessible in the home
