### Overview

We ensure that all foster carers receive regular and fair payments to
support the needs of the children in their care. Payments reflect the
level of responsibility and include allowances for everyday costs.

### ğŸ’¸ What Payments Cover

Foster carer payments include an allowance intended to cover:

-   Food, clothing, and personal care for the child
-   Pocket money and day-to-day expenses
-   Birthdays, holidays, and festive occasions
-   School-related costs, extracurricular and leisure activities
-   Day care if needed
-   Local transport (e.g. school, appointments, contact)

-   Foster carer training or professional development
-   Therapeutic or specialist services required by the child
-   Long-distance travel (with prior agreement)

Payments are made fortnightly by BACS. A year-end payment summary is

## Carers Must:

-   Inform home and motor insurers that they foster
-   Ensure foster children are covered by their policies
-   Hold appropriate buildings, contents, and fully comprehensive motor
    insurance
-   Ensure vehicles meet legal requirements (seatbelts, car seats, MOT)

## Wholistic's Support

We may contribute to the cost of repairs where damage is not covered by
your insurance and is due to fostering-related incidents. Decisions are
made on a case-by-case basis with appropriate evidence.

## Fostering Network Membership

All carers receive free membership to the Fostering Network, including:

-   Legal advice and support
-   Limited legal expenses cover for fostering-related matters
-   24-hour support line
