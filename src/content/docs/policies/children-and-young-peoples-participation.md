Participation is about making sure that children and young people have a
voice in the decisions that shape their lives. Wholistic Fostering
believes that meaningful involvement improves wellbeing, strengthens
care planning, and helps build confident, informed young people.

Children and young people are experts in their own lives. Listening to

essential for good care and better outcomes.

Wholistic Fostering is committed to:

-   Creating a culture where children are \*actively\* listened to
-   Involving them in decisions that affect their care and daily life
-   Ensuring participation is accessible, meaningful, and
    age-appropriate

Participation means more than simply asking questions. It includes:

-   Explaining why they are in foster care and what their rights are
-   Involving them in their Care Plans and everyday decisions
-   Supporting them to contribute to reviews and meetings
-   Helping them understand options and make informed choices
-   Providing access to advocacy and confidential support

## ğŸ’¬ How We Consult Children

Consultation should be:

-   **Regular** -- not just at reviews or when problems arise
-   **Accessible** -- using language and formats that work for each
    child
-   **Genuine** -- where views are taken seriously, not tick-box
    exercises
-   **Creative** -- using drawings, stories, exit interviews, or group
    chats

If decisions are made that go against their wishes, we explain why ---
and let them know how to raise concerns or challenge outcomes through
complaints or advocacy.
