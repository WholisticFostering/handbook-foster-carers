## ğŸ“¨ Enquiries & Information Packs

Anyone interested in fostering is treated with respect and given clear,
friendly information about what fostering involves. Within 5 working
days, enquirers receive:

-   A welcome pack about fostering
-   Application and consent forms
-   Invitation to an initial home visit if appropriate

Their details are recorded, including:

-   Name, DOB, address, phone
-   Household members
-   Bedroom availability
-   Fostering preferences
-   Previous experience

## ğŸ¡ Initial Visit

If suitable, an initial home visit is arranged within 5 working days.
This visit helps us:

-   Provide more detail about fostering
-   Discuss suitability and any early concerns
-   Agree next steps with the potential applicant

If fostering is not appropriate at this stage, the decision and reasons
are confirmed in writing, with guidance about how to challenge the
decision if needed.

## ğŸ“ Applying to Foster

Applicants complete:

-   An application form
-   Consent for health checks, references & background checks
-   A written declaration that the information is true

They are then booked onto the next available **Skills to Foster**
course.

## ğŸ“ Tools to Foster Training

Pre-approval training program - equivalent to skills to foster

All applicants attend this course to:

-   Understand the reality of fostering
-   Reflect on their suitability
-   Allow our team to observe their strengths

Attendance and participation form part of the overall assessment. If
concerns arise, applicants may be advised not to continue. They have a
right to challenge this decision through Panel.

## ğŸ” Checks and References

All adult household members and frequent visitors aged 18+ are subject
to:

-   Enhanced DBS (criminal records) checks
-   Local authority and health checks
-   Previous fostering/adoption agency checks
-   Employment references
-   Personal references (including a family member)
-   Ex-partner checks where relevant

Health checks via GP are reviewed by our Medical Adviser.

## ğŸ§‘â€ğŸ« Home Study / Assessment

A qualified social worker undertakes the assessment using **BAAF Form
F**. The assessment explores:

-   Parenting and life experience
-   Ability to provide a safe, nurturing home
-   Teamworking and communication
-   Understanding of safeguarding and behaviour

Family members are also interviewed. Accommodation is reviewed and
safety-checked.

Assessment includes observations from:

-   The \'Skills to Foster\' course
-   Statutory checks and references

The final report includes:

-   A summary of the applicant's strengths and development areas
-   Suggested approval terms (age range, number of children, etc)

Applicants have 28 days to comment before the report goes to Panel.

## ğŸ§‘â€âš–ï¸ The Fostering Panel

Applicants are invited to attend Panel, where:

-   The full report and checks are reviewed
-   Social workers present their assessment
-   Applicants can respond to the Panel's questions

Panel makes a **recommendation** to our Agency Decision Maker (ADM), who
confirms the final decision.

Applicants are told the outcome verbally within 24 hours, and receive
written confirmation within 5 working days.

## ğŸ›‘ What if the Application is Refused?

If the ADM intends to refuse, the applicant receives:

-   A written notice with reasons
-   Their right to challenge via:
    1.  Representations to Panel, or
    2.  Independent Review Mechanism (IRM)

The process must be completed within 28 days.
