This policy guides foster carers and staff in supporting positive

## ğŸŒ± Our Approach to Behaviour

We believe that positive relationships, understanding individual needs,
and creating a stable, caring environment are the keys to encouraging
good behaviour.

Foster carers are expected to:

-   Create a nurturing, calm and consistent household
-   Set clear, age-appropriate boundaries
-   Encourage positive choices through praise and rewards
-   Help children understand and take responsibility for their actions

## ğŸ‘ Encouragement and Reward

Carers should use everyday opportunities to:

-   Praise effort and achievements
-   Use reward systems where helpful (e.g. stickers, extra privileges)
-   Encourage participation in positive activities
-   Teach conflict resolution and emotional regulation

## ğŸš« Unacceptable Sanctions

The following are strictly not permitted:

-   Physical punishment (e.g. smacking, shaking)
-   Deprivation of food, drink, sleep or contact
-   Humiliation, ridicule or emotional abuse
-   Locking a child in a room or restricting movement without consent
-   Threats of removal from placement as punishment

Sanctions should always be:

-   Proportionate and appropriate to the child's understanding
-   Aimed at helping the child learn and grow
-   Logged and reviewed with your supervising social worker

## ğŸ¤ Supporting Positive Behaviour

We recognise some children have experienced trauma and may need
additional help. Foster carers will:

-   Work closely with their supervising social worker and the child's
    social worker
-   Take part in behaviour support planning
-   Use de-escalation techniques before any sanction is considered

## ğŸ›‘ Physical Intervention & Restraint

Physical restraint is only used:

-   As a last resort
-   When a child is at immediate risk of harming themselves or others
-   To prevent serious damage to property

If restraint is used:

-   It must be reasonable, proportionate and the minimum necessary
-   It must be reported immediately to the supervising social worker
-   A written report must be completed and reviewed

Foster carers will receive training on behaviour management and safe
holding techniques where required.

## ğŸ“ Recording and Oversight

Any use of restraint or serious behavioural incidents must be recorded

-   Your supervising social worker
-   The child's social worker
-   Senior management if required

This helps ensure transparency and safety for all involved.
