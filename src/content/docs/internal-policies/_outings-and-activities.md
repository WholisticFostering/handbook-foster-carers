This policy ensures that all outings and activities arranged by
Wholistic Fostering are planned, supervised, and reviewed to protect the
safety and well-being of everyone involved.

It applies to staff, volunteers, and participants.

## ğŸ“Œ Planning & Approval

**Risk Assessment**

-   Complete a risk assessment for each outing or activity --- see [Risk
    Assessment Template](resources/forms/outing_risk_template)
-   Identify hazards, risks, and mitigation steps

**Approval**

-   Seek approval from the [Registered
    Manager](staff/registered_manager) or [Operations
    Manager](staff/operations_manager)

**Permissions**

-   Obtain written consent from parents/carers for all external outings

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## ğŸ“ Preparation

**Itinerary**

-   Provide a full schedule to staff and participants before the trip
-   Include contact details, meeting points, and return times

**Supervision**

-   Ensure appropriate staff-to-child ratios based on needs and risks
-   Assign clear roles to each staff member

**First Aid**

-   At least one staff member must hold valid first aid certification
-   Carry a stocked first aid kit
-   Set emergency contact procedures in advance

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## ğŸš Transport

**Vehicle Safety**

-   Use insured and roadworthy vehicles (own or third-party)
-   Drivers must have a clean licence

**Supervision**

-   Maintain supervision during travel
-   Seat belts must be worn at all times

## ğŸ‘£ During the Outing

-   Staff, children, and volunteers must follow the [agency code of
    conduct](staff/code_of_conduct)

**Supervision**

-   Stay alert and engaged throughout the outing
-   Use a buddy system and headcounts to monitor group safety

**Behaviour Management**

-   Follow the [Behaviour Management
    Policy](policies/behaviour_management) when responding to any issues

## ğŸ¥ Health & Safety

**Medical Needs**

-   Check medical forms in advance for allergies, conditions, and

-   Carry necessary medications and instructions

**Safety Equipment**

-   Use helmets, life jackets, or other safety gear where required

**Weather & Environment**

-   Check forecasts and adapt plans as needed
-   Avoid known hazards or unsafe terrain

## ğŸ“£ Communication

**Pre-Trip Briefing**

-   Go over plans, rules, safety, and emergency info with all

-   Keep open communication between staff and young people
-   Ensure everyone knows who to contact if separated

**Post-Trip Debrief**

-   Hold a short staff debrief to evaluate how the outing went
-   Identify any lessons for next time
