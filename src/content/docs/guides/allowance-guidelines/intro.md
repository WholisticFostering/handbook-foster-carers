## ğŸ§’ Pocket Money & Savings

Pocket money must be paid to each child.

Amounts should be:
- Age appropriate
- Agreed at the Placement Planning Meeting

### ğŸ’¡ General Guidance

Typically:
- A percentage of the allowance is allocated to pocket money and savings
- The split increases with age
- Savings are encouraged for independence

Children should understand:
- The importance of saving
- That savings support independence
- That increased pocket money reduces savings

Any increase beyond standard guidance must be agreed with the Supervising Social Worker.

All direct payments to children must be:
- Recorded
- Signed for by both carer and child

## ğŸ“Š Tax & National Insurance

Foster carers are classed as self-employed.

A special tax scheme applies (often called Qualifying Care Relief).

In summary:

- There is a fixed annual tax threshold
- Additional weekly amounts per child increase the threshold
- If your fostering income stays below the threshold, no tax is due
- If income exceeds the threshold, tax may apply

You must:
- Register as self-employed
- Keep accurate records
- Complete a Self-Assessment Tax Return if required

Records must be kept for at least six years.

If unsure, seek professional financial advice.
