Pocket money is an important part of promoting independence, responsibility, and financial understanding.

It should be:

- Fair
- Age-appropriate
- Consistent
- In line with agency guidance

Pocket money must not be used as a reward for basic care or withheld as a punishment (except in clearly agreed and proportionate circumstances).

### ğŸ’¡ Teaching Financial Skills

Use pocket money as a learning opportunity:

- Help the child plan spending.
- Encourage saving for larger items.
- Discuss needs vs wants.
- Support opening a bank account (age appropriate).
- Introduce basic budgeting skills.

For older young people, consider:

- Digital banking awareness
- Online shopping safety
- Avoiding scams
- Responsible use of debit cards

### âš–ï¸ Sanctions & Deductions

Pocket money should not be:

- Stopped completely as punishment.
- Used to control behaviour.
- Withheld for minor issues.

If deductions are necessary (for example, deliberate damage to property):

- They must be proportionate.
- They must be explained clearly.
- They must be recorded.
- They must follow agency policy.

Basic allowances must not be removed in a way that undermines wellbeing.
