One of the key goals of fostering is to prepare children and young people for adulthood.

Promoting independence is about gradually developing:

- Confidence
- Life skills
- Decision-making ability
- Responsibility
- Emotional resilience

Independence should always be age-appropriate and based on individual needs.

- Self-esteem

### ğŸš¶ Safe Independence

Independence may include:

- Walking to school.
- Visiting friends.
- Using public transport.
- Staying home alone (where appropriate).

Any independence must:

- Be risk assessed.
- Be age-appropriate.
- Be agreed in the Placement Plan where required.

Children should not be left alone overnight unless formally agreed.
