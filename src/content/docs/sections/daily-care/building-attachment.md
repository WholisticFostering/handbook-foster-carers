Children in care may have experienced disrupted, inconsistent, or harmful early relationships.

Attachment is about helping a child feel:

- Safe
- Valued
- Understood
- Secure

Strong attachments support emotional regulation, behaviour, education, and long-term wellbeing.

Attachment develops when a child experiences:

- Consistent care
- Predictable responses
- Emotional warmth
- Reliable protection

Children who have experienced trauma may:

- Push adults away
- Appear overly independent
- Be overly clingy
- Struggle with trust
- Test boundaries repeatedly

These behaviours often reflect fear of rejection or abandonment.

All relationships experience conflict.

Attachment strengthens when:

- Adults apologise where appropriate.
- Conversations happen after emotions settle.
- The child knows the relationship is secure.

Avoid holding grudges or withdrawing emotionally.
