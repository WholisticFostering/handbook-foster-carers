### ğŸš« What Is Not Permitted

The following are not permitted:

- Physical punishment
- Corporal punishment
- Deprivation of food
- Humiliation
- Shouting in anger
- Locking children in rooms
- Withholding contact as punishment (unless agreed in care plan)

Sanctions must be reasonable, proportionate, and recorded.

Helpful approaches include:

- Positive reinforcement
- Praise and recognition
- Natural consequences
- Restorative conversations
- Clear routines
- Visual schedules (for younger children or those with additional needs)
- Time to calm down (not isolation)

Focus on repairing relationships after incidents.

### ğŸ—£ De-escalation Techniques

If behaviour escalates:

- Lower your voice.
- Reduce audience (move to a quieter space if safe).
- Offer choices.
- Acknowledge feelings.
- Give time and space.
- Avoid power struggles.

If there is immediate risk of harm, follow safeguarding procedures.

- Be reviewed.

Behaviour logs help identify patterns and improve support plans.
