title: Insurance & Liability
---

As a foster carer, you must ensure that appropriate insurance arrangements are in place.

Fostering changes the risk profile of your household.

You must inform your insurer that you are an approved foster carer.

Failure to notify your insurer may invalidate your policy.

## ğŸ  Home Insurance

You must ensure your home insurance:

- Covers fostering arrangements.
- Includes public liability cover.
- Covers accidental damage.
- Covers damage caused by children (where possible).

You should notify your insurer:

- When you are first approved.
- If placements change significantly.
- If household members change.

Keep written confirmation from your insurer.

If transporting children, your motor insurance must:

- Cover business use where required.
- Include appropriate passenger liability.
- Be valid and up to date.

Check your policy carefully.

If unsure, seek written confirmation from your insurer.

Children may accidentally or deliberately damage property.

Your home insurance may cover certain damage.

In some circumstances, the fostering service or local authority may have additional arrangements.

As a foster carer:

- You are expected to act reasonably and responsibly.
- You must follow agreed policies and risk assessments.
- You must not act outside delegated authority.

Failure to follow policy may affect liability arrangements.

Your fostering service may provide additional insurance cover, which may include:

- Public liability
- Allegation-related cover
- Legal advice access (where applicable)

You should familiarise yourself with:

- What is covered.
- What is not covered.
- How to make a claim.
- Reporting requirements.

## ğŸš¨ Reporting Incidents

If an incident occurs that may lead to an insurance claim:

1. Inform your Supervising Social Worker immediately.
2. Record the incident clearly.
3. Notify your insurer if required.
4. Follow agency guidance.

Do not admit liability without advice.

## ğŸ” Household Safety

Insurance does not replace safe practice.

You must:

- Maintain a safe home environment.
- Complete required safety checks.
- Follow risk assessment guidance.
- Update the agency about significant changes in your home.

Safe practice reduces risk and protects everyone.

## ğŸ›¡ Why This Matters

Under the Fostering Services (England) Regulations 2011 and National Minimum Standards:

- Foster homes must be safe.
- Appropriate insurance arrangements must be in place.
- Risk must be managed responsibly.

- Whether insurers have been notified.
- Whether carers understand liability.
- How damage or incidents are managed.
- Whether safety checks are completed.
