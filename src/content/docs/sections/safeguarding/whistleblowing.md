Whistleblowing is the process of raising concerns about unsafe, illegal, or unethical practice.

As a foster carer, you have a duty to speak up if you believe:

- A child is not being safeguarded.
- There is unsafe professional practice.
- There is misconduct or wrongdoing.
- Policies or procedures are not being followed.
- There is a risk of harm being ignored.

Raising concerns in good faith protects children â€” and you are protected when doing so.
