A child going missing is always a safeguarding concern.

Children in care are particularly vulnerable, and you must respond promptly, calmly, and in line with procedure.

Your role is to **take immediate action, inform the correct people, and record clearly**.

---

### üîé What Does "Missing" Mean?

A child is considered **missing from home** if:

- Their whereabouts are unknown, and  
- You are concerned for their safety or wellbeing.

This may include:

- Leaving without permission.
- Failing to return at an agreed time.
- Leaving during the night.
- Not returning from school or contact.

You do not need to wait a set period of time before acting.

---

### ‚ö° Immediate Actions

If a child is missing:

1. **Try to contact the child** (phone, text, social media if appropriate).
2. **Check the home and immediate surroundings.**
3. **Contact friends, known addresses, or usual locations** (if appropriate and safe).
4. Assess risk based on:
   - Age and vulnerability
   - Time of day
   - Weather conditions
   - Emotional state prior to leaving
   - Previous missing episodes
   - Risk of exploitation

If you are concerned about their safety at any point ‚Äî contact the police immediately.

---

### üöì Contacting the Police

Call **101** (or **999** if immediate danger).

Provide:

- Child‚Äôs full name and date of birth
- Physical description
- Clothing worn
- Last known location and time seen
- Known associates
- Mobile number
- Risk factors (self-harm, exploitation, medical needs)

Ask for:

- Incident reference number
- Officer name and collar number

Inform the police that the child is a **looked after child**.

### ‚òéÔ∏è Inform the Agency

Contact:

- Your Supervising Social Worker immediately, or  
- The Out-of-Hours service (if outside office hours)

Provide full details and police reference number.

Do not delay notifying the agency.

---

### üìù Recording Requirements

Record:

- Time child was discovered missing
- Actions taken
- Who was contacted
- Police reference number
- Communication with agency
- Time child returned
- Child‚Äôs presentation upon return

Keep records factual and timely.

---

### üß† When the Child Returns

When the child returns:

- Welcome them calmly.
- Ensure they are safe and well.
- Check for injuries.
- Offer food and reassurance.
- Do not interrogate or criticise.

Inform:

- The police (if they were involved)
- Your Supervising Social Worker

:::note
A **Return Home Interview** will be arranged by the local authority.
:::
---

### üíõ Support After a Missing Episode

Children may go missing due to:

- Emotional distress
- Trauma triggers
- Peer pressure
- Exploitation
- Conflict
- Fear

Remain supportive and avoid shaming.

Use the episode as an opportunity to:

- Explore what happened.
- Identify triggers.
- Update risk or safety plans if needed.

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Repeated Missing Episodes

If patterns develop:

- Inform your Supervising Social Worker immediately.
- Risk assessments may need updating.
- A strategy meeting may be required.
- Exploitation risks must be considered (CCE/CSE).

---

### üõ° Why This Matters

Under the Fostering Services (England) Regulations 2011 and National Minimum Standards:

- Missing episodes must be reported.
- Safeguarding risks must be assessed.
- Accurate records must be maintained.

Ofsted will review:

- Timeliness of reporting
- Risk assessment updates
- Evidence of supportive responses
