Police involvement may occur due to:

### âš¡ Immediate Actions

If police attend your home:

1. Remain calm and cooperative.
2. Ask for:
   - Officer name
   - Collar number
   - Police station
   - Incident reference number
3. Inform your Supervising Social Worker immediately.
4. If outside office hours, contact the Out-of-Hours service.

Do not delay informing the agency.

### ğŸ§’ If the Police Wish to Speak to the Child

Children in care have legal rights.

If police wish to interview a child:

- Inform your Supervising Social Worker immediately.
- A social worker should be notified.
- An Appropriate Adult must be present.
- The child has the right to legal representation.

You should not act as the Appropriate Adult unless specifically agreed and appropriate.

---

### ğŸš« If the Child Is Arrested

If a child is arrested:

- Inform your Supervising Social Worker immediately.
- Provide relevant medical or vulnerability information.
- Ensure the child has access to an Appropriate Adult.
- Record all details.

Children in care are considered vulnerable and must be treated accordingly.

### ğŸ›‘ Use of Police to Manage Behaviour

Police should **not** be used to manage routine behaviour issues.

Police involvement should be a last resort and only where:

- There is immediate risk of harm.
- A criminal offence has occurred.
- Serious property damage has taken place.

Normal behavioural challenges should be managed through restorative and trauma-informed approaches.

### ğŸ’› Supporting the Child Afterwards

Police involvement can be frightening or distressing.

After the incident:

- Offer reassurance.
- Maintain calm routines.
- Avoid shaming language.
- Support reflection in a restorative way.
- Update risk assessments if necessary.

Do not discuss police involvement with:

- Other carers

- Social media
