If a child experiences a serious injury or medical emergency, your priority is always **their immediate safety and wellbeing**.

Act quickly, remain calm, and follow clear procedures.

### ğŸš¨ When To Call 999

Call **999 immediately** if a child:

- Is unconscious or unresponsive
- Has difficulty breathing
- Is having a seizure lasting more than 5 minutes (or repeated seizures)
- Has severe bleeding that will not stop
- Has a suspected broken neck or back
- Has a serious head injury
- Has taken an overdose or ingested something harmful
- Is expressing immediate suicidal intent
- Has a severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
- Has chest pain or symptoms of stroke

Do not delay calling emergency services while seeking advice from others.
