A Personal Education Plan:

- Sets clear educational targets.
- Tracks academic progress.
- Identifies additional needs.

The PEP ensures that children in care do not fall behind.

## ğŸ¯ Setting Effective Targets

Targets should be:

- Specific
- Measurable
- Achievable
- Relevant
- Time-bound

Examples:

- Improve reading level by one stage within the term.
- Attend school 95% of the time.
- Complete homework independently twice per week.

Targets should focus on progress, not perfection.

## ğŸ’› Supporting the Child

PEP meetings should be child-centred.

Support the child by:

- Preparing them beforehand.
- Explaining the purpose in simple terms.
- Encouraging their voice.
- Avoiding overwhelming discussions.

Children should feel education is something done *with* them, not *to* them.
