School exclusions should be rare for children in care.

Exclusion can significantly impact:

- Educational progress
- Emotional wellbeing
- Placement stability
- Long-term outcomes

If an exclusion occurs, it must be addressed quickly and proactively.

There are two main types:

- Suspension (fixed-term exclusion)
- Permanent exclusion

Both must be reported immediately to the fostering service.

## üìù Understanding the Reason

You should:

- Ask for a clear explanation from the school.
- Request details of the incident.
- Clarify the length of the exclusion.
- Ask what steps are being taken to support reintegration.

Remain calm and professional in discussions.

---

## üè´ Reintegration Meetings

A reintegration meeting should take place following a suspension.

You should:

- Attend where possible.
- Share relevant context (if appropriate).
- Discuss strategies to prevent recurrence.
- Ensure a support plan is in place.

The focus should be on support, not blame.

---

## üß© Underlying Causes

Exclusions may relate to:

- Trauma triggers
- Attachment difficulties
- Special educational needs
- Emotional dysregulation
- Bullying
- Unmet learning needs

Addressing the root cause is essential.

---

## ü§ù Working With the School

Build constructive relationships with:

- Designated Teacher for Looked After Children
- SENCO
- Headteacher
- Pastoral staff

Discuss:

- Reasonable adjustments
- Behaviour support plans
- Additional learning support
- Risk assessments (if required)

Children in care should receive proactive support.

---

## üíõ Supporting the Child

After an exclusion:

- Remain calm and supportive.
- Avoid shaming language.
- Help them reflect on what happened.
- Focus on repairing relationships.
- Reinforce that education remains important.

Exclusion can feel rejecting or humiliating.

Your reassurance matters.

---

## üìö Educational Provision During Exclusion

During suspension:

- Work provided by the school should be completed.
- The child must be supervised during school hours.
- Encourage structured daytime routine.

Extended periods without education must be escalated.

---

## üìù Recording & Escalation

You must record:

- Date and type of exclusion.
- Reason given.
- Length of exclusion.
- Who was informed.
- Reintegration plans.

If exclusions become repeated or support is inadequate:

- Raise concerns with your Supervising Social Worker.
- Escalate if necessary.

---

## üõ° Why This Matters

Under the Fostering Services (England) Regulations 2011 and National Minimum Standards:

- Education must be actively promoted.
- Children in care should not be disproportionately excluded.
- Additional support must be provided where needed.

Ofsted will look for:

- Low exclusion rates.
- Evidence of carer involvement.
- Appropriate challenge to schools.
- Clear reintegration planning.
