Children in care are entitled to high-quality, consistent healthcare.

As a foster carer, you play a key role in ensuring children:

- Attend health appointments.
- Receive preventative healthcare.
- Have their health needs reviewed regularly.
- Are supported to understand their own health.

## ğŸ¥ Initial Health Assessment (IHA)

When a child enters care, an Initial Health Assessment (IHA) should take place.

This assessment:

- Reviews physical health.
- Reviews emotional wellbeing.
- Identifies unmet health needs.
- Establishes a health plan.

You may be asked to:

- Provide observations.
- Share known medical history.
- Attend the appointment.

---

## ğŸ”„ Review Health Assessments (RHA)

After the initial assessment, children must have regular Review Health Assessments:

- At least every 6 months for children under 5.
- At least annually for children over 5.

These reviews monitor:

- Growth and development.
- Immunisations.
- Dental care.
- Emotional wellbeing.
- Ongoing medical needs.

Attendance is essential.

Health reviews include emotional health.

- Mood changes.
- Sleep patterns.
- Appetite changes.
- School engagement.
- Signs of anxiety or low mood.

Concerns should be reported to your Supervising Social Worker.

Early support prevents escalation.

Under the Fostering Services (England) Regulations 2011 and National Minimum Standards:

- Children must receive regular health assessments.
- Health plans must be reviewed.
