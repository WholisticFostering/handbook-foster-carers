Children in care have the same right to healthcare as any other child.

However, consent for treatment can sometimes be more complex due to legal arrangements.

It is important that you understand:

- Who holds parental responsibility.
- What you are authorised to consent to.
- When to seek clarification.

## ğŸ“‹ Delegated Authority

Delegated authority outlines what decisions you can make without seeking additional consent.

This may include:

- Routine medical appointments
- Emergency treatment
- Dental check-ups
- Optician appointments
- Minor illnesses

Delegated authority must be clearly recorded in the Placement Plan.

You must not exceed delegated authority.

## ğŸ§’ Gillick Competence (Older Children)

Some young people may consent to their own medical treatment if assessed as competent.

This means they:

- Understand the treatment.
- Understand risks and benefits.
- Can weigh information appropriately.

Healthcare professionals assess competence.

You should inform your Supervising Social Worker if this situation arises.

- Update medication records.

Never agree to significant treatment without clarity on consent.
