All foster carers must be prepared to respond appropriately to minor injuries and medical emergencies.

At least one approved foster carer in the household must hold a valid First Aid certificate.

First aid knowledge protects children and may prevent situations from escalating.

You must:

- Hold a current, approved First Aid certificate.
- Renew training before expiry.
- Ensure all regular carers understand basic emergency procedures.
- Inform the agency if your certificate expires.

Training should include:

- CPR
- Choking response
- Managing bleeding
- Seizure response
- Allergic reactions
- Basic wound care

## ğŸ§° First Aid Kit

You must keep a fully stocked first aid kit that is:

- Easily accessible to adults.
- Out of reach of young children.
- Checked regularly.
- Replenished when items are used or expired.

If travelling, take a basic first aid kit with you.

## ğŸ©¹ Minor Injuries

For minor injuries:

- Clean wounds with appropriate materials.
- Apply dressings as needed.
- Monitor for signs of infection.
- Offer reassurance.
- Inform parents/social worker if required by placement agreement.
- Record the incident.

All injuries must be recorded, even if minor.

## ğŸš¨ When to Seek Medical Advice

Seek medical advice if:

- Bleeding does not stop.
- There is a head injury.
- There is loss of consciousness.
- A seizure lasts longer than expected.
- There are signs of concussion.
- A wound may require stitches.
- A child reports ongoing pain.
- You are unsure.

Head injuries must always be taken seriously.

Monitor for:

- Drowsiness
- Vomiting
- Headache
- Confusion
- Unequal pupils
- Behaviour changes

Seek medical advice if any symptoms appear.

Always record head injuries and inform the agency.

## ğŸ’‰ Allergic Reactions

Signs of a severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) include:

- Swelling of lips or throat
- Difficulty breathing
- Wheezing
- Sudden rash
- Collapse

If a child has an EpiPen:

1. Administer immediately.
2. Call 999.
3. Inform the agency.
4. Record the incident.

Do not delay emergency services.

---

## ğŸ§  Seizures

If a child has a seizure:

- Stay calm.
- Protect them from injury.
- Do not restrain.
- Do not put anything in their mouth.
- Time the seizure.
- Call 999 if it lasts longer than 5 minutes or if advised in care plan.

Follow any specific health care plan.

- Medical advice sought.

Serious incidents must be reported immediately to your Supervising Social Worker.

Promote hygiene by:

- Cleaning bodily fluids safely.

Protecting health protects everyone in the household.

- Follow-up support.
- Monitoring after the event.

Children with trauma may react strongly to medical situations.
