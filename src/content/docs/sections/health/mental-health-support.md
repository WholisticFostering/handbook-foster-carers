Children in care are more likely to experience emotional and mental health difficulties due to trauma, loss, and instability.

Promoting emotional wellbeing is just as important as supporting physical health.

You play a vital role in recognising concerns early and providing consistent emotional support.

## ğŸš¨ Self-Harm & Suicidal Thoughts

If a child:

- Talks about wanting to die
- Expresses hopelessness
- Engages in self-harm
- Has a plan to harm themselves

You must:

1. Take it seriously.
2. Stay calm and remain with the child.
3. Remove immediate risks if safe to do so.
4. Contact 999 if there is immediate danger.
5. Inform your Supervising Social Worker immediately.
6. Record clearly.

Never dismiss suicidal language as attention-seeking.
