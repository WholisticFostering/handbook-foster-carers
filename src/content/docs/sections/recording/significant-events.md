title: Significant Events & Incident Reporting
---

Certain events must be reported immediately to the fostering service.

Recording alone is not enough.

Significant events require:

- Immediate notification.
- Clear documentation.
- Follow-up action where required.

Prompt reporting protects children and protects you.

Significant events may include (but are not limited to):

- Safeguarding concerns or disclosures
- Allegations against you or a household member
- A child going missing
- Police involvement
- Serious injury or medical emergency
- Hospital admission
- Medication errors
- Criminal behaviour
- School exclusion (fixed-term or permanent)
- Placement at risk of breakdown
- Major behavioural incidents involving harm

## âš¡ Immediate Action Required

When a significant event occurs:

1. Ensure immediate safety.
2. Contact emergency services if required.
3. Inform your Supervising Social Worker immediately.
4. If outside office hours, contact the Out-of-Hours service.
5. Record the event factually.
6. Complete required incident forms.

Do not wait until the next working day.

---

## ğŸ“ What to Include in an Incident Report

Your report should include:

- Date and time of incident
- Who was present
- What happened (factually)
- Exact words spoken (if relevant)
- Action taken
- Who was informed
- Police or medical reference numbers (if applicable)
- Outcome at the time of recording

Reports must be factual, clear, and completed promptly.

---

## ğŸš« What Not to Do

Do not:

- Delay reporting.
- Attempt to investigate independently.
- Confront alleged individuals.
- Downplay serious incidents.
- Discuss the matter with unauthorised individuals.
- Post anything on social media.

Maintain professional boundaries at all times.

## ğŸ” Follow-Up & Review

After a significant event:

- Attend debrief meetings if required.
- Review Safer Care Plans.
- Update risk assessments.
- Participate in strategy discussions.
- Reflect during supervision.

Incidents should lead to learning and preventative planning.

## ğŸ” Confidentiality

Significant events must only be discussed with:

- Your Supervising Social Worker
- Relevant professionals
- Emergency services (if applicable)

Maintain strict confidentiality.

Under the Fostering Services (England) Regulations 2011 and National Minimum Standards:

- Clear and factual records.
- Appropriate escalation.
